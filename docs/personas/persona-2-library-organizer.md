# Persona 2: The Library Organizer

## Profile

**Name:** Daniel, 33  
**Occupation:** Product manager at a mid-size SaaS company  
**Location:** Austin, TX  
**Spotify since:** 2014  
**Playlists:** 60+  
**Device split:** 50% desktop (work), 50% mobile (commute, gym)  

---

## Who He Is

Daniel has been on Spotify since the early days and it shows — he has 60+ playlists that have accumulated like browser tabs. Some are great. Many are abandoned. A few he doesn't even remember making. He's a systems thinker by profession and it bothers him that his music library looks nothing like his actual life.

He has playlists for the gym, for deep work, for Sunday mornings, for cooking, for driving, for his kids. They're all named something vaguely useful but they're completely flat — no structure, no hierarchy, no way to know which "chill" playlist is the actual chill one.

He's not precious about music the way Maya is. He just wants it organized so he doesn't have to think about what to put on.

---

## Relationship with Music

- Music is a productivity and context tool, not an identity statement
- Different playlists for different activities — he's very intentional about this
- Doesn't discover music passively; he adds songs when they come up in conversation or movies
- Rarely edits playlists once built — he'd rather make a new one than prune an old one
- Uses music to signal to himself "this is work time" or "this is wind-down time"

---

## Current Pain Points with Spotify

- 60+ playlists in a flat list is unmanageable — can't find anything quickly
- No folder-like structure within Spotify's native app
- The "liked songs" playlist is a 2,000-song graveyard
- Spotify's recommendations are good but irrelevant to his use case (he wants his existing library organized, not expanded)
- Has playlists he built for old life contexts (college party playlist, ex-girlfriend's taste) that feel awkward to delete but weird to keep

---

## Why Playlistify Works for Him

- **Boards** are exactly the abstraction he needs: "Work", "Exercise", "Family", "Social"
- **Multi-select + add to board** lets him batch-organize his library in one session
- **Archive** is the right call for the playlists he doesn't want to delete but never uses
- **Search + filter** would help him find playlists when he needs them fast
- **Radio stations** concept appeals — he'd love a seamless "workout" station that pulls from his 3 gym playlists

---

## Potential Friction Points

- He'll try to use keyboard shortcuts and expect them to work
- He wants to search within a board, not just the whole library
- "Add playlists" from the board page opening a board-picker (instead of a playlist-picker) will confuse him immediately — he'll read it as broken
- He doesn't want to be prompted about AI features — he wants the tool to just work
- Radio is compelling but if it doesn't actually play music, he'll dismiss it entirely as vaporware

---

## Quote He'd Say About Playlistify

> "This is the thing Spotify should have built five years ago. I just need the 'add playlists to a board' button to actually work the way I expect."
