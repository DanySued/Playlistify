# Persona 3: The Social Listener

## Profile

**Name:** Zoe, 20  
**Occupation:** College sophomore, communications major  
**Location:** Chicago, IL  
**Spotify since:** 2021  
**Playlists:** 20  
**Device split:** 98% mobile  

---

## Who She Is

Zoe has been on Spotify for a few years but hasn't gone as deep as power users. She has about 20 playlists, most made for specific social moments: "pregame", "drive with the girls", "sad hours", "serotonin". She listens to music the way she uses social media — reactively, in the moment, and heavily tied to her current mood and social context.

She's aware that her library is kind of a mess but she doesn't feel the organizational urgency that Daniel does. What she wants is for music to feel *right* for the moment — and she often can't figure out which of her playlists fits right now.

---

## Relationship with Music

- Music is social glue and emotional language
- She sends songs to friends the way other people send memes
- A playlist for a specific night out or trip feels like a memory artifact — she can't delete it
- Heavily influenced by what's trending on TikTok and Instagram Reels
- Uses music to communicate her mood without having to say it explicitly

---

## Current Pain Points with Spotify

- Doesn't know what playlist to put on — the decision fatigue is real
- Can't always describe what she wants to hear but "knows it when she hears it"
- Her friends' playlists feel more curated than hers — comparison anxiety
- Spotify's radio recommendations feel random and impersonal
- The Spotify home screen shows her things she's already heard, not what fits *right now*

---

## Why Playlistify Works for Her

- **Discover page** is the most appealing feature — she wants to be prompted, not make decisions
- **Mood boards** ("sad hours", "hype", "chill") match how she already thinks about playlists
- **Visual masonry grid** feels more like TikTok/Pinterest than a file manager — familiar UX
- **Labels** could let her tag playlists by energy level or social context
- **Board concept** would help her separate "playlists I made for events" vs "playlists for myself"

---

## Potential Friction Points

- She's 98% mobile and the entire app is desktop-first — this is a deal-breaker unless fixed
- The Discover page mood prompt cards don't do anything — this is the feature she'd use most
- She won't understand what "boards" means from the sidebar icon alone — needs onboarding
- She won't configure Gemini API keys — AI features need to be zero-setup for her
- Radio creation is too many steps — she wants "make me something that fits this mood" not a configuration panel
- She won't read the "Note: only approved testers can log in" disclaimer — she'll think the app is broken

---

## Quote She'd Say About Playlistify

> "Wait this looks so much better than Spotify's app. Why can't I use it on my phone though?"
