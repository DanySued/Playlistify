# Persona 1: The Aesthetic Curator

## Profile

**Name:** Maya, 24  
**Occupation:** Graphic design student / freelance illustrator  
**Location:** Brooklyn, NY  
**Spotify since:** 2017  
**Playlists:** 45+  
**Device split:** 80% mobile, 20% desktop  

---

## Who She Is

Maya treats her Spotify library the way other people treat their Instagram grid — with intent. Every playlist has a cover she designed herself, a name that sounds like a short film title, and a vibe that holds together from first track to last. She's had "Late Night Architecture" in rotation for three years. She doesn't skip songs from it.

She works from coffee shops and listens constantly — while sketching, commuting, cooking. Music is ambient to her work, not separate from it.

She has strong opinions about what counts as a "real" playlist (curated, not algorithmic) and doesn't trust Spotify's Discover Weekly to understand her.

---

## Relationship with Music

- Music organizes her emotional states, not just her time slots
- Playlists are personal artifacts — she'd feel uncomfortable sharing her "3am sad" playlist publicly
- She judges people (gently) by their music taste
- She's the friend everyone asks to make the playlist for the road trip, the dinner party, the breakup

---

## Current Pain Points with Spotify

- No visual way to see all playlists at once — the list format feels like a filing cabinet
- No way to group playlists by mood or season without renaming them all
- Custom covers she uploads disappear or get overridden
- Can't see playlist "vibes" at a glance — just alphabetical names in a list
- Collaborative playlists feel chaotic — no curation control

---

## Why Playlistify Works for Her

- **Masonry grid** immediately appeals — it's how she already thinks about music collections
- **Boards by mood** maps to how she naturally organizes: "dark academia", "summer 2023", "productive mornings"
- **Image upload in Edit modal** — she wants her cover art to show up correctly
- **AI name suggestions** — she'll use this ironically and seriously in equal measure
- **Label system** — she'll create labels like "chill", "high energy", "for car", "no skip"

---

## Potential Friction Points

- She organizes on mobile 80% of the time — the desktop-first design may frustrate her
- She'll want to reorder playlists within a board (drag to arrange)
- She'll notice immediately if the gradient colors clash or feel random
- If she can't upload a custom board cover, she'll feel the board "isn't hers"
- The "Discover" page mood cards that do nothing will confuse and annoy her

---

## Quote She'd Say About Playlistify

> "It finally looks like how I think about my playlists. I just need it to work on my phone too."
